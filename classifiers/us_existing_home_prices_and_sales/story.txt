US existing home sales leap 6.1% in March.
After sluggish existing home sales at the start of the year, housing activity picked up sharply in March.
Sales of existing homes increased 6.1% in March to a seasonally adjusted rate of 5.19 million, the National Association of Realtors said April 22. The median price in March for existing homes stood at $212,100, NAR reported, which is a 7.8% increase from March 2014.
Nationwide, home prices were up 4.6% in January compared to the same month in the previous year. The S&P/Case-Shiller index found the biggest year-on-year gains in home prices were in Denver and Miami, where prices rose 8.4 and 8.3%, respectively.
Six cities, including San Francisco, reported price declines in the twelve months leading up to January. Home prices rose only 7.9% in San Francisco, down from an annual rate in 2014 of 9.4%.
Overall sales in 2014 reached 4.93 million, a 3.1% decline compared to sales in the previous year. In addition, housing inventory continued to shrink, slipping 11.1% in December 2014 to 1.85 million existing homes available for sale compared to November 2014.
